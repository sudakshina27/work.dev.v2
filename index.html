About

Learning to build things that feel quietly meaningful.

I’ve always been drawn to understanding how things work beneath the surface — whether that meant mathematics, systems, music, or pieces of code that refused to cooperate.

I started my career working with backend systems and Salesforce at Deloitte, where I spent time building APIs, solving operational problems, and contributing to internal tools used at scale. Somewhere along the way, I found myself growing curious about AI, not just the technology itself, but the strange and fascinating ways humans are trying to teach machines to think.

Right now, I’m pursuing a Master’s in Artificial Intelligence Engineering and learning as I go — slowly, properly, and with intention. I don’t know everything yet, and I think that’s what makes this phase exciting.

Lately, I’ve been interested in building small projects that teach me something in return. Things that are useful, a little personal, and occasionally experimental.

Outside of tech, I spend a lot of time singing, reading fiction, working out, and disappearing for sunsets whenever I can.
